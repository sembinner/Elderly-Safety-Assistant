Privacy Policy

Elderly Safety Assistant

Last updated: 2026-07-04

1. Who this policy covers

Elderly Safety Assistant ("the app") has two linked roles:

  • Client: the person being kept safe. Their device shares location, battery status, and SOS alerts with their caregivers.
  • Caregiver: a person monitoring one or more clients. They see the client's status, manage safe zones, and add notes, appointments, and medications.

A client and caregiver are connected through a one-time pairing code or QR code. A caregiver may be linked to multiple clients, and a client may be linked to multiple caregivers; all linked caregivers can see the same client data unless a note is marked private.

If you are a caregiver setting up the app on behalf of someone who cannot give their own consent (for example, due to a cognitive condition), you confirm that you are authorized to do so and that you will explain the app's monitoring to them to the extent possible.

2. Information we collect

Account

The app does not ask for an email address, phone number, or password. Each device signs in with an anonymous account, identified only by a randomly generated ID. Pairing links a client's anonymous account to a caregiver's anonymous account.

Profile

A display name and, optionally, a profile photo you choose from your photo library. These are shown to linked caregivers/clients so people can tell who is who.

Location

With permission, the client's precise GPS location (including in the background, when the app is closed or the phone is locked) so caregivers can see the client's last known position. Location updates are paired with the device's battery level and charging state so caregivers know if a phone has died or lost connectivity.

SOS alerts

When a client taps the SOS button, an alert is created and, where available, a snapshot of their location at that moment is attached.

Safe zones (geofences) and boundary events

Caregivers can draw a safe-zone circle around a location (e.g. home). When a client's device enters or exits a zone, an event is recorded and linked caregivers are notified.

Notes, appointments, and medications

Caregivers can add free-text notes (optionally marked private, visible only to the author), appointments (title, location, notes, date/time), and medication entries (name, dosage, schedule, notes) for a client. This data can include health-related information — only enter what is necessary for care coordination.

Camera

Used only to scan a pairing QR code during setup. No photo or video is captured or stored.

Push notification token

A device token used to deliver alerts (SOS, safe-zone events) when the app is not open.

Automatically collected technical data

Standard operational data such as timestamps of updates and, if you use a hosted backend, infrastructure logs (e.g. IP address, request metadata) generated by our hosting and push-notification providers as part of normal service operation.

We do not collect analytics, advertising identifiers, or run any ad or tracking SDKs.

3. End-to-end encryption — what's protected and what isn't

During pairing, the client and caregiver devices exchange public keys and the client's device seals a symmetric key to each linked caregiver. Using that key, the following are encrypted on the device before they are ever sent to our servers, and can only be decrypted by paired caregiver devices — we cannot read them, even if our database were compromised:

  • Live location updates
  • The location snapshot attached to an SOS alert
  • Safe-zone (geofence) definitions, including names and coordinates
  • Safe-zone entry/exit events

Push notifications themselves are content-free: they carry only an alert type and internal record ID, never a coordinate or zone name. The receiving app fetches and decrypts the details locally.

Notes, appointments, and medications are currently stored unencrypted in our database, protected by access controls that restrict them to the client and their linked caregivers. If your device is compromised or you use a public/shared device, keep this in mind before entering sensitive details in these fields.

4. How we use this information

  • To show a caregiver their linked client's live location, battery/connectivity status, and safe-zone status.
  • To deliver SOS and safe-zone alerts to linked caregivers.
  • To let linked caregivers coordinate care through shared notes, appointments, and medications.
  • To operate, secure, and troubleshoot the app (e.g. diagnosing a failed pairing or push delivery).

We do not sell personal data, and we do not use it for advertising.

5. Who we share information with

  • Linked caregivers and clients. Data described above is shared only with accounts you have actively paired with, as described in Section 1.
  • Service providers who process data on our behalf, under contracts limiting their use of it to providing the service to us:
    • Supabase — hosts our database, authentication, realtime sync, and server-side functions.
    • Firebase Cloud Messaging (Google) — delivers push notifications to Android/iOS devices.
  • Legal reasons. We may disclose information if required by law, or to protect the safety of a user or the public (for example, responding to a genuine emergency).

We do not otherwise share personal data with third parties.

6. Data retention

Location updates, SOS alerts, and safe-zone events are retained to support the care-monitoring history caregivers rely on. Notes, appointments, and medications persist until deleted by their author or the client's account is removed. When a client account is deleted or a pairing is removed, associated location, alert, and safe-zone data tied to that link is deleted or becomes inaccessible to the unlinked caregiver.

7. Data security

We use encryption in transit (TLS) for all network requests, and end-to-end encryption for the location, SOS-location, and safe-zone data described in Section 3. Database access is restricted by row-level security policies scoped to the authenticated device/account. No security measure is perfect, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Your choices and rights

  • Permissions: you can deny or revoke camera, location, and photo-library permissions in your device settings at any time; doing so will disable the corresponding feature (QR scanning, location sharing, or profile photos).
  • Background location: a client can turn this off in device settings, which will stop location sharing with caregivers.
  • Safe-zone notifications: can be turned off in the app; SOS alerts cannot be turned off, as they exist for emergencies.
  • Unlinking: a client or caregiver can remove a pairing at any time, which stops further data sharing between those two accounts.
  • Deletion: you can request deletion of your account and associated data by contacting us (Section 11). Because accounts are anonymous, we may ask you to confirm deletion from the device itself.
  • Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights (e.g. access, correction, portability, or objection) under laws such as the GDPR or CCPA/CPRA. Contact us to exercise these.

9. Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children and is not intended for use by children as a caregiver account holder. A client profile may belong to any age group requiring care and supervision; caregivers are responsible for appropriate use in that case.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app changes. We will update the "Last updated" date above and, for material changes, provide notice in the app.
